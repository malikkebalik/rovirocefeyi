Meg Ryan’s Return to the Silver Screen

Meg Ryan, a leading actress of her time, is making her comeback to the silver screen with her upcoming film, “What Happens Later.” In addition to her role as Willa in the movie, Ryan also took on the responsibilities of directing and writing.

The idea for “What Happens Later” came to Ryan during the pandemic, as she reflected on the concept of two people being stuck together. She expressed her love for the idea of being held in a space, even if it feels conflicted, as it can lead to healing.

Despite her multiple roles in the project, Ryan found acting to be the easiest part. She expressed her desire to direct again, as she enjoyed the experience of being in the director’s chair and acknowledged that there may have been things she missed while focusing on her acting role.

During the filming of “What Happens Later,” Ryan and her team worked within a tight budget of $3 million. They had to complete the movie quickly and even used real people as extras. In post-production, they made adjustments to ensure consistency in the visuals, utilizing digital techniques.

Reflecting on her break from the spotlight, Ryan explained that she took time off to develop other aspects of her life as a human being. She also shared her admiration for actor Kevin Costner, with whom she has had a spark whenever they have crossed paths at showbiz events. Ryan is patiently waiting for the drama surrounding Costner’s divorce to settle down, as she hopes to work with him in a lead role in the future.
