Dave Portnoy changes Barstool Radio, rips 'delusional' Kelly Keegs

September 13, 2024
blog

Explore More

Dave Portnoy had enough.

Taking to his X account, the Barstool Sports founder pulled the plug on Barstool Radio on Monday, and explained that he wasn’t happy with hosts Kelly Keegs and Francis Ellis.

Now, he’s replacing — at least part-time — the pair with himself and fellow Barstool personalities, Kirk Minihane and and Ryan Whitney.

“Breaking – A new once a week Barstool Radio/DPS show will be launching soon hosted by @kirkmin with myself and @ryanwhitney6,” Portnoy posted Tuesday on X.

“All parties have committed to 42 weeks a year or until somebody quits the company.”

The day prior, Portnoy cancelled Barstool Radio “until further notice.”

Portnoy explained on the “Barstool Rundown” that he “had no choice but to cancel” Barstool Radio due to Keegs and Francis spending most of the episode talking about Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce, instead of discussing Keegs’ feud with fellow Barstool employee, Ohio’s Tate.

“These two might be the most tone deaf, brain dead, idiotic…,” Portnoy said. “Francis and Kelly are the wrong people. Go back to blogging.”

Keegs and Ohio’s Tate were engulfed in a social media feud last week, when they dragged each other in blog posts on Barstool Sports’ website and various posts on X.

Keegs said Ohio’s Tate questioned her work ethic when he said she hasn’t blogged on Fridays, and wasn’t happy about her comments about her makeup.

“I don’t know if Kelly Keegs was joking about makeup and not being here on Fridays,” Portnoy said while explaining the situation on the Barstool Rundown. “I didn’t even know she doesn’t show up on Fridays.

“She just does not blog from home, so canceled. Those two aren’t cut out for it … Go back to blogging or doing whatever. You’re not cut out for Barstool Radio, you’ll never be back on it. You’re out.”

Keegs addressed the cancellation and called it a sad situation.

“Nothing else I can say,” Keegs wrote on X. “The world needs ditch diggers too I guess.”

In a follow-up post, Keegs wrote: “Sad. I wanted to turn Barstool Radio into something fun and positive for us all. Everyone else just wants to stay at each other’s throats nonstop. I’m not that girl. I’ve got one weekly fight in me, at best. Sounds like a job for @kirkmin.”

Portnoy replied to Keegs in a series of posts Tuesday on X.

“In honor of being fair to both sexes at Barstool I must say this is top 3 most delusional tweets I’ve ever seen and I don’t know what the other 2 are off the top of my head,” Portnoy wrote.

Keegs appeared to move on from the drama with Barstool Tate.

“Unfortunately now that I’ve learned @BarstoolTate is literally just a troll who regurgitates internet comments as content, I’m no longer interested in this beef,” she wrote Friday in a post on X.

“Read his blog, read my blog response, who gives a s–t. He will always be a massive f–king loser lol.”
