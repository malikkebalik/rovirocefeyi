Carlton Pearson- Professional Career

Carlton Pearson is a Christian minister and vocalist from the United States. In 1981, he established Higher Dimensions Evangelistic Center, which has grown to be one of Tulsa’s largest churches. Along with Dr. Frederick K. C. Price, he was one of only two African American ministers to broadcast a weekly national television preaching show. He had a weekly audience ranging from tens of thousands to millions. He’s also known for being one of the first black ministers in the country to hold huge conferences in arenas and stadiums. In the 1990s, his church grew to a weekly average of over 6,000 people.

In addition, in 2000, the minister conducted a campaign for George W. Bush and was eventually welcomed into the White House. Pearson’s TV show was one of Trinity Broadcasting Network’s most popular. In Tulsa, he also hosted the AZUSA Conference. The preacher also worked as a traveling evangelist, holding two-day revivals around the country. He also gave some up-and-coming pastors and singers national and international exposure. In November 2006, he was ordained as a United Church of Christ minister.

The New Dimensions Worship Center, as it was named at the time, moved its services to Tulsa’s All Souls Unitarian Church in June 2008. He has also published several books and booklets. He recently felt driven to preach the “Gospel Of Inclusion,” and has penned the critically acclaimed Gospel Of Inclusion, published by Simon & Schuster.
