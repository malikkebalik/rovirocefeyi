Jim Gaffigan Early Life/Career

Early Life: - On July 7, 1966, in Elgin, Illinois, James Christopher Gaffigan was born, the youngest of six children to peace activist and fundraiser Marcia Miriam and banker Michael Ambrose Gaffigan. He is of Irish heritage, and his surname was originally Gavahan. Iowa Supreme Court Justice Richard F. Mitchell was his maternal grandfather. Gaffigan grew up in Chesterton, Indiana, and frequently makes jokes about growing up in a large family. His mother was a skilled needleworker who got a national award from the American Needlepoint Guild in 1985 for unique design and craftsmanship. She died of cancer in 1990, at the age of 53.

Professional Career: - Gaffigan traveled to New York to pursue a career in comedy in 1990, driven by his respect for fellow Hoosier David Letterman. He got a job in advertising and worked during the day while taking acting training at night. During this time, he was cast in a series of advertisements for Blockbuster Video called "Entertainment Squad." His career took off after a classmate dared him to join a stand-up seminar that demanded a live set at the conclusion. He fell in love with stand-up comedy and began performing at comedy clubs regularly till the early hours of the morning, following his evening acting courses. He was frequently discovered napping on the job, and his employer had to shake him up in order to terminate him.
